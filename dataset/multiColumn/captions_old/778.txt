 The average cost of rent to household income ratio in the United Kingdom (UK) remained consistent between October 2018 and October 2019. London had the highest household rent to income ratio despite a slight decrease between October 2018 and October 2019. The North East of England saw the lowest rent to income ratio across the UK of 23.7 percent in October 2019. Slight reprieve for renters Things got a little easier on renters across many parts of the United Kingdom. The North West saw the largest decrease in rent to income, falling by 1.5 percent as of October 2019. At the other end of the scale, the West Midlands saw an average increase of 0.7 percent. Cost of rent continues to rise As of September 2019, the average cost of rent in Greater London increased by over 50 British pounds as compared to the same period of 2018.
