 The unemployment rate for people ages 16 to 24 in the United States in 2019 was 8.4 percent. However, this rate was much lower for people aged 45 and over, at 2.7 percent. U.S. unemployment The unemployment rate in the United States varies based on several factors, such as race, gender, and level of education. African-Americans had the highest unemployment rate in 2019 out of any ethnicity, and people who had less than a high school diploma had the highest unemployment rate by education level. Alaska is consistently the state with the highest unemployment rate, although the El Centro, California metropolitan area was the area with the highest unemployment rate in the country in 2018.
