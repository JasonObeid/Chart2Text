Tequila is a distilled spirit made from the blue agave plant. The beverage takes its name from the city of Tequila, the area around which is one of the primary production locations. Tequila is traditionally produced with between 35 to 55 percent alcohol and must be at least 80 proof in the United States to be considered tequila by law.
