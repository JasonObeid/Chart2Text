 Since 2005, the gross minimum wage per hour in France has been gradually increasing. In June 2005, the gross minimum wage per hour in France amounted to 8.03 euros. Fourteen years later the hourly minimum wage increased by two euros, reaching 10.03 euros. In 2017, France had an average annual wage higher than countries like Spain and Italy, but lower than those in Germany or in the United Kingdom. The issue of purchasing power In recent years purchasing power has become one of most worrying issues of households in France. In 2015, a large majority of French perceived their purchasing power as decreasing, while four years later it became one of the main the causes of the Yellow Vests protests in the country. Studies have shown that the purchasing power in the country reached its lowest level between 2010 and 2014. Household expenditure doubled since 1990, while 52.4 percent of French middle-income households stated having difficulties to make ends meet in 2017.
