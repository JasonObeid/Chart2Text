 With over half a million students, comprehensive schools accounted for the largest educational institution in Finland in 2018. These schools provide compulsory education and include primary schools (grades 1 to 6) and secondary schools (grades 7 to 9). The region of Uusimaa with the largest amount of pupils Uusimaa, the region with the capital city Helsinki, encompasses the highest number of comprehensive schools in the country in 2018. Therefore, with roughly 168 thousand pupils, the region also had the largest amount of children attending primary and secondary schools. In the beginning of autumn of 2018, nearly 19 thousand new pupils began their comprehensive education in Uusimaa.
