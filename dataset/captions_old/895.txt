 As of January 2020, the price of rubber amounted to 1.68 U.S. dollars per kilogram. The average annual price of natural rubber at the Singapore Commodity Exchange (one of the key global commodity exchanges for rubber) reached a high of 4.82 U.S. dollars per kilogram in 2011. Since then, the price decreased to an annual average of 1.64 U.S. dollars per kilogram in 2019. Rubber prices and rubber producers Rubber is a widely used material, in both naturally and synthetically sourced forms. Companies use it in the manufacturing of tires and tubes, rubber gloves, latex condoms, erasers, in adhesives and coatings, nozzles, as elastic material in clothing, and more. Accordingly, rubber is an important commodity due to its numerous uses in everyday items.
