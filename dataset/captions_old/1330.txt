 In 2019, shoppers aged between 45 and 64 accounted for the majority of pet store revenue with a 43.5 percent share, and therefore made up the largest target market in the United States. Those aged between 25 and 44 made up the second largest market, providing 33.1 percent of pet store revenue in the same year. Continued importance of brick-and-mortar stores in the pet market Ninety-three percent of cat owners and 88 percent of dog owners continue to purchase pet food in physical storesin the U.S. These figures decrease by around 10 percent when talking about shoppers who exclusively purchase in pet stores. Despite this, a substantial share of pet owners still visit pet stores, regardless of the rise of e-commerce in the United States. Pet superstores are the most visited type of pet product outlet among both dog and cat owners, followed by pet specialty chains.
