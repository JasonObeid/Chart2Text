 In 2018, there were around 5,084 choking deaths in the United States. Death from choking is more common among the elderly with food most often responsible for such incidents. Death from choking In the United States, the odds of one dying from choking on food is around 1 in 2,696. These odds are greater than the odds of dying from an accidental gun discharge or as a passenger on a plane. In 2018, there were around 1.6 deaths from choking per 100,000 population.Choking among children Choking is also hazardous among young children.
