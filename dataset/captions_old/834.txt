 This statistic shows the average net profit margin of the global mining industry represented by the top forty mining companies worldwide, from 2002 to 2018. In 2011, the net profit margin of the mining industry's leading companies was approximately 24 percent. Seven years later, in 2018, the net profit margin stood at ten percent. The net profit margin (also known as profit margin, net margin, net profit ratio) is a measurement to describe the profitability of a company. It is calculated by dividing the net income by the total revenue (or net profit by sales). For 2018, it means that the top 40 mining companies kept ten cents of profit out of every U.S. dollar they earned.
