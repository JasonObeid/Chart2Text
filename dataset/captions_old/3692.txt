Why is it so wet in Scotland? With its mountainous landscape, it is no coincidence that on average it is Scotland that receives the most annual rain in the UK. The wettest parts of the UK are generally in mountainous regions, with the Western Highlands prone to high levels of rain. Here, rainfall can be 3,000mm per year. However, the East of Scotland can see levels as low as 800mm. This is often due to rainfall from the Atlantic weather systems coming in from the West and as these systems move east, rain deposits reduce.
