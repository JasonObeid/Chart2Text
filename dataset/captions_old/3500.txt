 The Royal bank of Scotland (RBS) is part of The Royal Bank of Scotland Group plc with Natwest and Ulster Bank and consists of 67 thousand employees over 187 branches across the United Kingdom. As of 2017, RBS had over 830 billion euros in assets, the fourth highest in the United Kingdom. History of RBS Founded in Edinburgh in 1727, the Royal bank of Scotland is an amalgamation of hundreds of past banks. As the second bank to be established in Scotland, it initially traded only within its home country up until 1783, opening its first branch in Glasgow in the same year. Banks are well known as acquirers, as they constantly look to strengthen their financial positioning and swallow those that could compete with. In 1864, RBS made its first acquisition, buying the Dundee Banking Co.
